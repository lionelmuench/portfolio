Projects

01

"Overhead"

Rectilinear & Curvilinear

In these studies, I learned how to create balance between shapes by simplifying forms into dominant, subdominant, and subordinate forms. We created small sculptures and modeled them in Rhino.

02

"Prism"

Planar

In this study of planar materials, we learned to work with bends and folds to put 2-dimensional shapes into 3 dimensions. My group learned about material strength, and using alternative methods of holding besides glue and tape. We applied lessons of hierarchy from our previous projects to create a visually appealing model.

03

"Whistle"

Lines in Space

Here, we learned how about how curves take up space and the relationship between multiple curves. My final project was inspired by the path of an untied balloon taking flight when released. I applied my knowledge of material strength from the planar project; I experimented with doubling and tripling wire until the lines had the weight and strength I wanted them to.

04

"Encore"

Concavity & Convexity

In this project, I learned to project top and side views onto a 3-dimensional shape and envision how a top and side cut would turn into a complex shape. My shape was inspired by a performer with hands in the air, hence the title of "Encore". I also improved my skills in woodworking.

Final Project

"Tymbal"

These devices, inspired by the cicada, aim to make sound through buckling.

Concept

I was inspired by the sequential buckling of a cicada's ribs to create noise, as well as the sequential noise-making of a grager. I was also moved by the steel drum's use of alternative materials, bringing me to use a tape measure.

Process

I first created a simple mechanism as a proof-of-concept for the tape measure's ability to create noise. After this, I studied mechanisms that turn rotational force to sequential or linear force, bringing me to use cams to sequentially buckle each tape measure. From here, I tweaked the length of the cams, the shape of the device's body, and the handle design for better user experience.
